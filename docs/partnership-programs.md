# Partnership & Affiliate Programs

Running notes on third-party rideshare / mobility affiliate and partnership
programs CBL could plug into. The pattern most of these follow is **CPA**
(cost-per-action — flat payout when a referred user completes their first
ride), **not** revenue share on every ride. If CBL wanted ongoing per-ride
commission from these platforms, that model isn't publicly offered.

Most useful as a **fallback monetization** layer: if a CBL member is in a
market where we don't have driver coverage yet, surface a partner option
with our affiliate code rather than losing the user to a browser tab.

---

## Uber

Managed through the Impact affiliate network. Apply at
<https://www.uber.com/us/en/affiliate-program/>.

- **$5** per new rider who completes their first trip.
- **$30–$50** per new driver who completes their first trip after activation.
- 30-day cookie window.
- One-time payout per signup, no revenue share.

### Uber for Business (separate program)

- **$50 voucher** (not cash) per referred *organization* that signs up and
  spends $100 within 60 days.
- Cap of $500 total per account per 3-month window.
- US only.
- Only useful if CBL pursues corporate accounts.

### Uber Eats Affiliate

- Separate program, for restaurants. Not relevant to ride monetization.

---

## Lyft

Managed through CJ Affiliate, FlexOffers, and other networks. Less
standardized than Uber's — terms vary noticeably by network and region.

- **$5–$6** per new rider (typical) when they complete their first eligible
  ride.
- Some networks have historically offered higher one-time bonuses
  (e.g., one source cited "$35 commission + $5 first ride" via VigLink),
  but those promo terms shift frequently.
- Cap of **~$2,000** per account per period.
- Same CPA model — no ongoing per-ride share.

---

## Waymo

No public affiliate program at Uber's tier. Coverage is geographically
selective, so this would be a **city-specific fallback** rather than
universal.

### Coverage (mid-2026)

Direct Waymo service in: Dallas, Houston, Los Angeles, Miami, Nashville,
Orlando, Phoenix, San Antonio, San Francisco + surrounding Bay Area.
Additionally available in **Atlanta and Austin via Waymo's Uber partnership**.

### Programs available

- **Personal referral:** $10 off first ride for new users; referrer earns
  Waymo credit (not cash). Consumer tier only.
- **No open affiliate signup.** Partnerships happen via BD — see the
  [Lyft × Waymo Nashville launch](https://investor.lyft.com/news-events-presentations/press-releases/detail/89/lyft-and-waymo-launch-partnership-to-expand-autonomous-mobility-to-nashville)
  as the template for what a platform deal looks like.
- Pitch path: <https://waymo.com/community/partners/>

### Why Waymo is a strategically different partner

Autonomous-only — does **not** compete with CBL's independent-contractor
drivers the way Uber/Lyft do. Best framed as "spare capacity" in cities where
CBL doesn't have driver coverage yet, rather than a head-to-head rideshare
alternative.

---

## Strategic notes

- All three programs reward signups, not ongoing ride volume. Treat affiliate
  income as a small one-time stream, not a recurring channel.
- Surfacing these inside CBL actively **competes** with the core CBL ride
  product. Worth gating to specific conditions (no nearby CBL driver, off-app
  context, etc.) rather than putting partner links on primary surfaces.
- The CBL App as the ride-booking surface remains the primary monetization
  model — these are supplements.
